Type 2 diabetes care, made simple

Board-certified endocrine care in Elyria & Westlake — and by televisit across Ohio.

Request Appointment Call (440) 322-0872

Living with type 2 diabetes means a lot of small decisions every day — what to eat, when to test, whether that number on the meter is something to worry about. Our job is to make those decisions simpler. We build a plan around your life, explain everything in plain language, and adjust as you go.

Dr. Vikram Kumar and our team have cared for people with diabetes in Northern Ohio since 2009, with offices in Elyria and Westlake and televisits available across Ohio.

How we manage type 2 diabetes

Every plan starts with understanding where you are: your A1C (which we test in-house, so you get results at your visit), your medications, your habits, and what's realistic for you. From there we fine-tune — that might mean adjusting oral medications, discussing newer options like GLP-1 medications, adding continuous glucose monitoring so you can see your patterns, or simply making sense of the plan you already have.

Follow-ups don't always need a drive: many of our patients handle routine check-ins by secure televisit from home.

When to see an endocrinologist for type 2 diabetes

Primary care doctors handle a lot — but diabetes often benefits from a specialist who works with it every day. That's what we do. It may be time to see a specialist if your A1C stays high despite treatment, your medications keep changing without clear results, you're experiencing lows, or you simply want a clear, coherent plan from a team that will explain the "why" behind it.

Common questions

Do I need a referral to be seen for type 2 diabetes?
Many insurance plans let you book directly with a specialist; some require a referral from your primary care doctor. Call us at (440) 322-0872 and we'll help you check what your plan needs.
Can you take over my diabetes medications from my family doctor?
Yes. We coordinate with your primary care physician — we manage the diabetes plan in detail and keep them informed, so your care stays joined-up.
Do you offer A1C testing at the office?
Yes — we run A1C in-house, so results are ready during your visit instead of days later.
Are GLP-1 medications an option for type 2 diabetes?
For many patients, yes. Whether they fit your situation depends on your health history and insurance coverage — it’s a conversation we’ll have at your visit.
