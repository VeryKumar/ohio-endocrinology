### What are GLP-1 medications?
GLP-1 receptor agonists — including semaglutide (Ozempic®, Wegovy®) and tirzepatide (Mounjaro®, Zepbound®) — mimic a natural hormone your body releases after eating. They help your pancreas release insulin when it's needed, quiet the constant hunger signals, and slow digestion so you feel full longer. The result for many patients: meaningfully lower A1C, steady weight loss, and better cardiovascular health.

These are powerful medications — and they work best with a specialist watching the whole picture. As endocrinologists, GLP-1s aren't a side offering for us. They're part of the metabolic care we've practiced for decades.

### Why manage GLP-1s with an endocrinologist?
- **Right medication, right dose.** We match the medication to your labs, your history, and your goals — and adjust the dose so results come without unnecessary side effects.
- **The full metabolic picture.** In-house A1C testing and continuous glucose monitoring let us see exactly how your body is responding — not just what the scale says.
- **Side effects handled early.** Nausea and digestive issues are the main reason people quit GLP-1s. We titrate carefully and stay reachable, so small problems stay small.
- **Insurance & affordability guidance.** ⚠️ confirm practice actually assists with PAs/savings programs.
- **Muscle-preserving weight loss.** Losing weight isn't the goal — losing the right weight is.

### Who is a candidate?
- Type 2 diabetes that isn't at goal on current medication
- A BMI of 30+, or 27+ with a weight-related condition ⚠️ confirm criteria
- Prediabetes or insulin resistance with difficulty losing weight ⚠️ confirm scope
- A history of "trying everything" without lasting results

Some conditions (personal/family history of medullary thyroid cancer, MEN 2, pancreatitis, pregnancy) mean GLP-1s aren't appropriate. Your first visit includes a full review.

### FAQ
- **Do you prescribe Ozempic and Mounjaro, or compounded versions?** We prescribe FDA-approved GLP-1 medications through your pharmacy. ⚠️ confirm stance on compounded semaglutide.
- **Will my insurance cover it?** Often yes for type 2 diabetes; coverage for weight loss alone varies. ⚠️ confirm.
- **How much weight will I lose?** In clinical trials, patients lost on average 15% of body weight on semaglutide and up to 20% on tirzepatide over about a year.
- **What are the side effects?** Most common: nausea, constipation, fatigue — especially when the dose increases. Text us at (440) 656-7755 when something feels off.
- **I'm already on insulin — can I still use a GLP-1?** Often yes — and it may reduce how much insulin you need.
- **Do I have to stay on it forever?** GLP-1s treat a chronic condition; they don't cure it.
